Introduction

I am not an Entrepeneur of any sort and I am definitely not an early adopter when it comes to  technology. I sincerely feel that there should be some category beyond Laggards so that I  could place myself. Call it 'pathetic depressive clinically laggard'  or something that sort. I am a computer programmer by profession and to quite an extent by  choice. So I need to keep up with some information which other people generally would not - e.g. What are the different new  features which came in C++ language in the latest revision. That I  think is 'call of duty not' something ingraind in me. I still use a mobile phone which has just the basic features like  call and SMS. The camera in it takes photograph with such a low resolution that it is impossible to tell whether that is a  human face or a horse. No wonder I don't use its camera. My only excuse all along had been that I don't need anything other  that call and SMS in my phone. Though I had an account for years I avoided Facebook for a long time  believing that it is a of waste of my time.  Little did I know that world has gone a long way in all these years  riding on things like Facebook and Smart phones.

First step for Entrepreneurship - looking for problems

As it is mentioned countless times before and recently asserted by Paul Graham in his article  that the way to get startup  ideas is to look for problems. Being an early adopter helps a big time spotting problems when a new  technology wave is coming in. For a laggard that means that he has  to look for problems which all the early adopters have overlooked. There are people who starts a wave by themselves - they are the innovaters. They give a solution and unless you start using it, you did not know that the problem existed. Dropbox is an example which worked for me that  way. Before it I used to sync my different computers using email or  flash drives and I used to think that, that is the best way possible.

Adoption not everything - execution is

Yes, there had been Myspace before Facebook, but Facebook executed beautifully and  today I don't know anyone in my circle who uses Myspace actively. So it is very much possible that people who come a little late in the technology wave and execute better to make a mark. Having said that early adopters definitely have an edge. They are the people who can most likely transition to a technology entrepreneurship role. Who can forget that DHH used a language Ruby, which was quite unheard of by many people in the Web domain at that time. That is a classic case of early adoption. The rest, as everyone knows, is history.
